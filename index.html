Dr. Gandhi is a leading education researcher, author and activist. Across her many organizations and schools, she is working towards quality education for all.

Her Unique Story

Dr Sunita Gandhi, an Indian educator, researcher, and author, is CEO of Dignity Education Vision International (DEVI Sansthan), and Chief Academic Advisor to the largest school in the world (City Montessori School). A Physics PhD from Cambridge University, Dr. Gandhi served as a Young Professional and Economist at the World Bank. She is the author of Disruptive Literacy: A Roadmap for Urgent Global Action (Bloomsbury, 2022) and Accelerating Learning for All: A Groundbreaking Pedagogy to Transform Education (Bloomsbury, 2024). Her recognitions include India's Educationist of the Year 2014, Visionary of Uttar Pradesh 2017, and Shining World Caring Award 2020, Taiwan. A lifelong educationist, she has visited 50 countries, studying the education systems of many. She founded her first school at the age of 14, for underprivileged children. Dr. Gandhi’s integrity, determination and grit makes her an extraordinary educator.

With years of research in Indian slums and schools, Dr. Gandhi developed Accelerating Learning for All (ALfA), a groundbreaking, fast, and holistic educational pedagogy, replicated in 33 languages. Since its launch in 2022, ALfA has witnessed exponential growth in India and other countries, with pilots underway in twelve countries along with state-wide adoption by Himachal Pradesh. A chapter in a recent book edited by Prof. Fernando Reimers of Harvard Graduate School of Education documents ALfA’s highly significant results after just 40 days of implementation.

Under the guidance of her parents, Dr Jagdish Gandhi and Dr Bharti Gandhi, who are the founders of the world’s largest school, CMS, Dr Sunita Gandhi’s childhood days offered her a great scope of learning. When she was growing up, her family’s finances were constrained, but she was always enthusiastic about her study. Her empathy, compassion and kindness towards the poor have always been strong; at the age of 14, she cracked the NTSE examination and started school for the underprivileged. Meeting Saint Mother Teresa around this time was a life-changing opportunity for her. The saint had blessed her, saying, “God loves Sunita. Love others as He loves you.”

At 16, her parents sent her to study in the UK on donations from friends and family. A few years later, she completed a doctorate in Physics from Cambridge University on generous academic scholarships. After one year as a scientist at Lawrence Berkeley Laboratories, USA, she applied to the most unlikely position of an economist through the prestigious Young Professionals Program of the World Bank. Her selection meant she became a permanent member of the World Bank fraternity. She worked on education policy and development programs in many countries, including a participatory project for women in Morocco. This project was showcased at the UN Summit in Copenhagen and led to her receiving the World Bank's most outstanding contribution to development award.

After ten years at the World Bank, she was at the peak of her career when she decided to quit this highly prestigious job and go back to India to work on education reform, with a particular focus on the underprivileged.

Over the years, she has visited 50 countries on six different continents. She has studied their education systems and exchanged notes with educators and policymakers worldwide. She has been fortunate enough to share these ideas with educators worldwide, including at Finland's annual Educator's conference on invitation from the Finnish National Board of Education. Dr Gandhi leads the Disruptive Education Movement through initiatives like Ed Leadership Conferences, GETI Live talks, and Teachers’ Training Programs to transform India’s education system, as envisaged by the New Education Policy, 2020 and the NIPUN Bharat Initiatives.

Her efforts in education have spanned a vast range, from the bottom-up grassroots level in Indian slums and villages through her NGO to establishing her schools. She has established Iceland's first two charter schools, an experimental school in the Czech Republic, and 19 schools in India. She has spoken at several conferences on five continents, including recently in BETT Asia. Dr Gandhi was invited to Finland as the Keynote to the largest gathering of their educators by the Finnish National Board of Education.

Her research work in Iceland, the UK and India mainstream the principle of 'compete with yourself' versus 'compete with others', published in a 2017 book by Palgrave Macmillan, UK. Today, over 1000 schools in India and overseas have adopted her curriculum and assessments based on this principle. With this large-scale pilot, she is fine-tuning an education more suited to the needs of children living in the 21st century. The foundation of her education is constructed on Four Building Blocks of Education: Universal Values, Global Understanding, Excellence in All Things and Service to Humanity.

Dr. Gandhi’s latest project, Global Dream Accelerating Learning for All, aims at providing foundational literacy and numeracy to all. Dr. Gandhi’s remarkable literacy materials have been used in thousands of schools.

The ALfA program can teach how to read, write, and do basic arithmetic in just three months, rather than the three years required in conventional schooling and adult literacy programs. This is only possible through a groundbreaking pedagogy.

  • Upside-down literacy. Flipping the conventional ‘A for Apple’ approach on its head, ALfA uses ‘Apple: /A/’. Working from known pictures to unknown words, students take turns to ask each other three simple questions: “What is this picture?” “What is its first sound?” and “What word do these sounds make together?”
  • Paired Learning. Instead of a transaction of learning from teacher to taught, learning is co-constructed by the students themselves, working in pairs. The emphasis shifts from teaching to learning by discovery and by interaction with each other.
  • Innovative Curriculum. Instead of the normal text-heavy, teacher-centered materials, ALfA study materials consist of short, visual modules that flow scientifically and are designed for paired and independent learning.
  • Cooperative Assessments. The purpose of assessment is not to judge or label students, but rather to enable children to reflect, thereby becoming more self-aware. Instead of competing against other students, ALfA assessments measure students’ progress compared to their own baseline.
  • Magic Mathematics. ALfA uses a concrete-to-abstract approach to math. Children develop excellent number sense by representing numbers and solving questions using any locally available counters such as matchsticks (ones) and ice cream sticks (tens).
